Linear Mathematics
Part IB, 2003

(a) Let U,U′U, U^{\prime}U,U′ be subspaces of a finite-dimensional vector space VVV. Prove that dim⁡(U+U′)=dim⁡U+dim⁡U′−dim⁡(U∩U′).\operatorname{dim}\left(U+U^{\prime}\right)=\operatorname{dim} U+\operatorname{dim} U^{\prime}-\operatorname{dim}\left(U \cap U^{\prime}\right) .dim(U+U′)=dimU+dimU′−dim(U∩U′).

(b) Let VVV and WWW be finite-dimensional vector spaces and let α\alphaα and β\betaβ be linear maps from VVV to WWW. Prove that

rank⁡(α+β)⩽rank⁡α+rank⁡β\operatorname{rank}(\alpha+\beta) \leqslant \operatorname{rank} \alpha+\operatorname{rank} \beta rank(α+β)⩽rankα+rankβ
