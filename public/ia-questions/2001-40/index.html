2.II.11F

#Probability
2001

Dipkomsky, a desperado in the wild West, is surrounded by an enemy gang and fighting tooth and nail for his survival. He has mmm guns, m>1m>1m>1, pointing in different directions and tries to use them in succession to give an impression that there are several defenders. When he turns to a subsequent gun and discovers that the gun is loaded he fires it with probability 1/21 / 21/2 and moves to the next one. Otherwise, i.e. when the gun is unloaded, he loads it with probability 3/43 / 43/4 or simply moves to the next gun with complementary probability 1/41 / 41/4. If he decides to load the gun he then fires it or not with probability 1/21 / 21/2 and after that moves to the next gun anyway.

Initially, each gun had been loaded independently with probability ppp. Show that if after each move this distribution is preserved, then p=3/7p=3 / 7p=3/7. Calculate the expected value ENE NEN and variance Var NNN of the number NNN of loaded guns under this distribution.

[Hint: it may be helpful to represent NNN as a sum ∑1≤j≤mXj\sum_{1 \leq j \leq m} X_{j}∑1≤j≤m​Xj​ of random variables taking values 0 and 1.]
