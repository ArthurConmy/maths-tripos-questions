#Probability
2004

Define the covariance, cov⁡(X,Y)\operatorname{cov}(X, Y)cov(X,Y), of two random variables XXX and YYY.

Prove, or give a counterexample to, each of the following statements.

(a) For any random variables X,Y,ZX, Y, ZX,Y,Z

cov⁡(X+Y,Z)=cov⁡(X,Z)+cov⁡(Y,Z)\operatorname{cov}(X+Y, Z)=\operatorname{cov}(X, Z)+\operatorname{cov}(Y, Z) cov(X+Y,Z)=cov(X,Z)+cov(Y,Z)

(b) If XXX and YYY are identically distributed, not necessarily independent, random variables then

cov⁡(X+Y,X−Y)=0\operatorname{cov}(X+Y, X-Y)=0 cov(X+Y,X−Y)=0
