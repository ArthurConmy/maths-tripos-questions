Silver Beard the space pirate believed people relied so much on space-age navigation techniques that he could safely write down the location of his treasure using the ancient art of vector algebra.   5. Vector d=(1,−2,2)\mathbf{d}=(1,-2,2)d=(1,−2,2).

  6. Vector e\mathbf{e}e has length a⋅b\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}a⋅b. Spikey was initially a little confused with this one, but then realised the orientation of the vector did not matter.
