#Vectors and Matrices
2019

If AAA is an nnn by nnn matrix, define its determinant det⁡A\operatorname{det} AdetA.

Find the following in terms of det⁡A\operatorname{det} AdetA and a scalar λ\lambdaλ, clearly showing your argument:

(i) det⁡B\operatorname{det} BdetB, where BBB is obtained from AAA by multiplying one row by λ\lambdaλ.

(ii) det⁡(λA)\operatorname{det}(\lambda A)det(λA).

(iii) det⁡C\operatorname{det} CdetC, where CCC is obtained from AAA by switching row kkk and row l(k≠l)l(k \neq l)l(k=l).

(iv) det⁡D\operatorname{det} DdetD, where DDD is obtained from AAA by adding λ\lambdaλ times column lll to column kkk (k≠l)(k \neq l)(k=l).
