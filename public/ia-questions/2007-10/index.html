Paper 3, Section II, D

#Algebra and Geometry
2007

Let AAA be a real symmetric n×nn \times nn×n matrix. Prove that every eigenvalue of AAA is real, and that eigenvectors corresponding to distinct eigenvalues are orthogonal. Indicate clearly where in your argument you have used the fact that AAA is real.

What does it mean to say that a real n×nn \times nn×n matrix PPP is orthogonal ? Show that if PPP is orthogonal and AAA is as above then P−1APP^{-1} A PP−1AP is symmetric. If PPP is any real invertible matrix, must P−1APP^{-1} A PP−1AP be symmetric? Justify your answer.

Give, with justification, real 2×22 \times 22×2 matrices B,C,D,EB, C, D, EB,C,D,E with the following properties:

(i) BBB has no real eigenvalues;

(ii) CCC is not diagonalisable over C\mathbb{C}C;

(iii) DDD is diagonalisable over C\mathbb{C}C, but not over R\mathbb{R}R;

(iv) EEE is diagonalisable over R\mathbb{R}R, but does not have an orthonormal basis of eigenvectors.
