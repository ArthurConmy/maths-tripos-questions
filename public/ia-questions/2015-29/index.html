Paper 4, Section II, E

#Numbers and Sets
2015

What does it mean for a set to be countable? Prove that

(a) if BBB is countable and f:A→Bf: A \rightarrow Bf:A→B is injective, then AAA is countable;

(b) if AAA is countable and f:A→Bf: A \rightarrow Bf:A→B is surjective, then BBB is countable.

Prove that N×N\mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N}N×N is countable, and deduce that

(i) if XXX and YYY are countable, then so is X×YX \times YX×Y;

(ii) Q\mathbb{Q}Q is countable.

Let C\mathcal{C}C be a collection of circles in the plane such that for each point aaa on the xxx-axis, there is a circle in C\mathcal{C}C passing through the point aaa which has the xxx-axis tangent to the circle at aaa. Show that C\mathcal{C}C contains a pair of circles that intersect.
