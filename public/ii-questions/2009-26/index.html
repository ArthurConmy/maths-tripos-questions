Paper 1, Section I, H

#Coding and Cryptography
2009

I am putting up my Christmas lights. If I plug in a set of bulbs and one is defective, none will light up. A badly written note left over from the previous year tells me that exactly one of my 10 bulbs is defective and that the probability that the kkk th bulb is defective is k/55k / 55k/55.

(i) Find an explicit procedure for identifying the defective bulb in the least expected number of steps.

[You should explain your method but no proof is required.]

(ii) Is there a different procedure from the one you gave in (i) with the same expected number of steps? Either write down another procedure and explain briefly why it gives the same expected number or explain briefly why no such procedure exists.

(iii) Because I make such a fuss about each test, my wife wishes me to tell her the maximum number NNN of trials that might be required. Will the procedure in (i) give the minimum NNN ? Either write down another procedure and explain briefly why it gives a smaller NNN or explain briefly why no such procedure exists.
