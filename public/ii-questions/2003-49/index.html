A1.4

#Groups, Rings and Fields
2003

(i) Let ppp be a prime number. Show that a group GGG of order pn(n⩾2)p^{n}(n \geqslant 2)pn(n⩾2) has a nontrivial normal subgroup, that is, GGG is not a simple group.

(ii) Let ppp and qqq be primes, p>qp>qp>q. Show that a group GGG of order pqp qpq has a normal Sylow ppp-subgroup. If GGG has also a normal Sylow qqq-subgroup, show that GGG is cyclic. Give a necessary and sufficient condition on ppp and qqq for the existence of a non-abelian group of order pqp qpq. Justify your answer.
