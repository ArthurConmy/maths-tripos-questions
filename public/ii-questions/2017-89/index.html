State the Completeness Theorem for Propositional Logic.

[You do not need to give definitions of the various terms involved.]

State the Compactness Theorem and the Decidability Theorem, and deduce them from the Completeness Theorem.

A set SSS of propositions is called finitary if there exists a finite set TTT of propositions such that {t:S⊢t}={t:T⊢t}\{t: S \vdash t\}=\{t: T \vdash t\}{t:S⊢t}={t:T⊢t}. Give examples to show that an infinite set of propositions may or may not be finitary.

Now let AAA and BBB be sets of propositions such that every valuation is a model of exactly one of AAA and BBB. Show that there exist finite subsets A′A^{\prime}A′ of AAA and B′B^{\prime}B′ of BBB with A′∪B′⊨⊥A^{\prime} \cup B^{\prime} \models \perpA′∪B′⊨⊥, and deduce that AAA and BBB are finitary.
