4.II.19F

#Representation Theory
2006

In this question, all vector spaces will be complex.

(a) Let AAA be a finite abelian group.

(i) Show directly from the definitions that any irreducible representation must be one-dimensional.

(ii) Show that AAA has a faithful one-dimensional representation if and only if AAA is cyclic.

(b) Now let GGG be an arbitrary finite group and suppose that the centre of GGG is nontrivial. Write Z={z∈G∣zg=gz∀g∈G}Z=\{z \in G \mid z g=g z \quad \forall g \in G\}Z={z∈G∣zg=gz∀g∈G} for this centre.

(i) Let WWW be an irreducible representation of GGG. Show that Res⁡ZGW=dim⁡W⋅χ\operatorname{Res}_{Z}^{G} W=\operatorname{dim} W \cdot \chiResZG​W=dimW⋅χ, where χ\chiχ is an irreducible representation of ZZZ.

(ii) Show that every irreducible representation of ZZZ occurs in this way.

(iii) Suppose that ZZZ is not a cyclic group. Show that there does not exist an irreducible representation WWW of GGG such that every irreducible representation VVV occurs as a summand of W⊗nW^{\otimes n}W⊗n for some nnn.
