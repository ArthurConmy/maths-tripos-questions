#Geometry and Groups
2011

Let AAA and BBB be two rotations of the Euclidean plane E2\mathbb{E}^{2}E2 about centres aaa and bbb respectively. Show that the conjugate ABA−1A B A^{-1}ABA−1 is also a rotation and find its fixed point. When do AAA and BBB commute? Show that the commutator ABA−1B−1A B A^{-1} B^{-1}ABA−1B−1 is a translation.

Deduce that any group of orientation-preserving isometries of the Euclidean plane either fixes a point or is infinite.
