Paper 4, Section II, I

#Representation Theory
2016

Let NNN be a proper normal subgroup of a finite group GGG and let UUU be an irreducible complex representation of GGG. Show that either UUU restricted to NNN is a sum of copies of a single irreducible representation of NNN, or else UUU is induced from an irreducible representation of some proper subgroup of GGG.

Recall that a ppp-group is a group whose order is a power of the prime number ppp. Deduce, by induction on the order of the group, or otherwise, that every irreducible complex representation of a ppp-group is induced from a 1-dimensional representation of some subgroup.

[You may assume that a non-abelian ppp-group GGG has an abelian normal subgroup which is not contained in the centre of GGG.]
