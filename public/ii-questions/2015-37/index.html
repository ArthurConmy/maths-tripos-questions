#Differential Geometry
2015

Let U(n)\mathrm{U}(n)U(n) denote the set of n×nn \times nn×n unitary complex matrices. Show that U(n)\mathrm{U}(n)U(n) is a smooth (real) manifold, and find its dimension. [You may use any general results from the course provided they are stated correctly.] For AAA any matrix in U(n)\mathrm{U}(n)U(n) and HHH an n×nn \times nn×n complex matrix, determine when HHH represents a tangent vector to U(n)\mathrm{U}(n)U(n) at AAA.

Suppose that HHH represents a tangent vector to U(n)\mathrm{U}(n)U(n) at the identity matrix III. Sketch an explicit construction of a geodesic curve on U(n)\mathrm{U}(n)U(n) passing through III and with tangent direction HHH, giving a brief proof that the acceleration of the curve is always orthogonal to the tangent space to U(n)\mathrm{U}(n)U(n).
