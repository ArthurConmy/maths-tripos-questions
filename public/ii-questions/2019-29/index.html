#Classical Dynamics
2019

A simple harmonic oscillator of mass mmm and spring constant kkk has the equation of motion

mx¨=−kx.m \ddot{x}=-k x . mx¨=−kx.

(a) Describe the orbits of the system in phase space. State how the action III of the oscillator is related to a geometrical property of the orbits in phase space. Derive the action-angle variables (θ,I)(\theta, I)(θ,I) and give the form of the Hamiltonian of the oscillator in action-angle variables.

(b) Suppose now that the spring constant kkk varies in time. Under what conditions does the theory of adiabatic invariance apply? Assuming that these conditions hold, identify an adiabatic invariant and determine how the energy and amplitude of the oscillator vary with kkk in this approximation.
