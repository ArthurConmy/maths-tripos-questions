Paper 1, Section II, I

#Representation Theory
2019

(a) State and prove Schur's lemma over C\mathbb{C}C.

In the remainder of this question we work over R\mathbb{R}R.

(b) Let GGG be the cyclic group of order 3 .

(i) Write the regular RG\mathbb{R} GRG-module as a direct sum of irreducible submodules.

(ii) Find all the intertwining homomorphisms between the irreducible RG\mathbb{R} GRG-modules. Deduce that the conclusion of Schur's lemma is false if we replace C\mathbb{C}C by R\mathbb{R}R.

(c) Henceforth let GGG be a cyclic group of order nnn. Show that

(i) if nnn is even, the regular RG\mathbb{R} GRG-module is a direct sum of two (non-isomorphic) 1dimensional irreducible submodules and (n−2)/2(n-2) / 2(n−2)/2 (non-isomorphic) 2-dimensional irreducible submodules;

(ii) if nnn is odd, the regular RG\mathbb{R} GRG-module is a direct sum of one 1-dimensional irreducible submodule and (n−1)/2(n-1) / 2(n−1)/2 (non-isomorphic) 2-dimensional irreducible submodules.
