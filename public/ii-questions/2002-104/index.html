B4.7

#Hilbert Spaces
2002

Throughout this question, HHH is an infinite-dimensional, separable Hilbert space. You may use, without proof, any theorems about compact operators that you require.

Define a Fredholm operator TTT, on a Hilbert space HHH, and define the index of TTT.

(i) Prove that if TTT is Fredholm then im⁡T\operatorname{im} TimT is closed.

(ii) Let F∈B(H)F \in \mathcal{B}(H)F∈B(H) and let FFF have finite rank. Prove that F∗F^{*}F∗ also has finite rank.

(iii) Let T=I+FT=I+FT=I+F, where III is the identity operator on HHH and FFF has finite rank; let E=im⁡F+im⁡F∗E=\operatorname{im} F+\operatorname{im} F^{*}E=imF+imF∗. By considering T∣ET \mid ET∣E and T∣E⊥T \mid E^{\perp}T∣E⊥ (or otherwise) prove that TTT is Fredholm with ind T=0T=0T=0.

(iv) Let T∈B(H)T \in \mathcal{B}(H)T∈B(H) be Fredholm with ind T=0T=0T=0. Prove that T=A+FT=A+FT=A+F, where AAA is invertible and FFF has finite rank.

[You may wish to note that TTT effects an isomorphism from (ker⁡T)⊥(\operatorname{ker} T)^{\perp}(kerT)⊥ onto im⁡T\operatorname{im} TimT; also ker TTT and (im⁡T)⊥(\operatorname{im} T)^{\perp}(imT)⊥ have the same finite dimension.]

(v) Deduce from (iii) and (iv) that T∈B(H)T \in \mathcal{B}(H)T∈B(H) is Fredholm with ind T=0T=0T=0 if and only if T=A+KT=A+KT=A+K with AAA invertible and KKK compact.

(vi) Explain briefly, by considering suitable shift operators on ℓ2\ell^{2}ℓ2 (i.e. not using any theorems about Fredholm operators) that, for each k∈Zk \in \mathbb{Z}k∈Z, there is a Fredholm operator SSS on HHH with ind S=kS=kS=k.
