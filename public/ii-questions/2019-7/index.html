#Algebraic Topology
2019

In this question, XXX and YYY are path-connected, locally simply connected spaces.

(a) Let f:Y→Xf: Y \rightarrow Xf:Y→X be a continuous map, and X^\widehat{X}X a path-connected covering space of XXX. State and prove a uniqueness statement for lifts of fff to X^\widehat{X}X.

(b) Let p:X^→Xp: \widehat{X} \rightarrow Xp:X→X be a covering map. A covering transformation of ppp is a homeomorphism ϕ:X^→X^\phi: \widehat{X} \rightarrow \widehat{X}ϕ:X→X such that p∘ϕ=pp \circ \phi=pp∘ϕ=p. For each integer n⩾3n \geqslant 3n⩾3, give an example of a space XXX and an nnn-sheeted covering map pn:X^n→Xp_{n}: \widehat{X}_{n} \rightarrow Xpn​:Xn​→X such that the only covering transformation of pnp_{n}pn​ is the identity map. Justify your answer. [Hint: Take XXX to be a wedge of two circles.]

(c) Is there a space XXX and a 2-sheeted covering map p2:X^2→Xp_{2}: \widehat{X}_{2} \rightarrow Xp2​:X2​→X for which the only covering transformation of p2p_{2}p2​ is the identity? Justify your answer briefly.
