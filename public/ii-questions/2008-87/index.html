1.II.23H

#Riemann Surfaces
2008

Define the terms Riemann surface, holomorphic map between Riemann surfaces and biholomorphic map.

Show, without using the notion of degree, that a non-constant holomorphic map between compact connected Riemann surfaces must be surjective.

Let ϕ\phiϕ be a biholomorphic map of the punctured unit disc Δ∗={0<∣z∣<1}⊂C\Delta^{*}=\{0<|z|<1\} \subset \mathbb{C}Δ∗={0<∣z∣<1}⊂C onto itself. Show that ϕ\phiϕ extends to a biholomorphic map of the open unit disc Δ\DeltaΔ to itself such that ϕ(0)=0\phi(0)=0ϕ(0)=0.

Suppose that f:R→Sf: R \rightarrow Sf:R→S is a continuous holomorphic map between Riemann surfaces and fff is holomorphic on R\{p}R \backslash\{p\}R\{p}, where ppp is a point in RRR. Show that fff is then holomorphic on all of RRR.

[The Open Mapping Theorem may be used without proof if clearly stated.]
