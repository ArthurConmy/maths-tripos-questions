4.II.21F

#Algebraic Topology
2008

Let XXX and YYY be topological spaces.

(i) Show that a map f:X→Yf: X \rightarrow Yf:X→Y is a homotopy equivalence if there exist maps g,h:Y→Xg, h: Y \rightarrow Xg,h:Y→X such that fg≃1Yf g \simeq 1_{Y}fg≃1Y​ and hf≃1Xh f \simeq 1_{X}hf≃1X​. More generally, show that a map f:X→Yf: X \rightarrow Yf:X→Y is a homotopy equivalence if there exist maps g,h:Y→Xg, h: Y \rightarrow Xg,h:Y→X such that fgf gfg and hfh fhf are homotopy equivalences.

(ii) Suppose that X~\tilde{X}X~ and Y~\tilde{Y}Y~ are universal covering spaces of the path-connected, locally path-connected spaces XXX and YYY. Using path-lifting properties, show that if X≃YX \simeq YX≃Y then X~≃Y~\tilde{X} \simeq \tilde{Y}X~≃Y~.
