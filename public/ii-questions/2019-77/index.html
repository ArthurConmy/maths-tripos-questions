Paper 2, Section II, 17G

#Graph Theory
2019

(a) Suppose that the edges of the complete graph K6K_{6}K6​ are coloured blue and yellow. Show that it must contain a monochromatic triangle. Does this remain true if K6K_{6}K6​ is replaced by K5K_{5}K5​ ?

(b) Let t⩾1t \geqslant 1t⩾1. Suppose that the edges of the complete graph K3t−1K_{3 t-1}K3t−1​ are coloured blue and yellow. Show that it must contain ttt edges of the same colour with no two sharing a vertex. Is there any t⩾1t \geqslant 1t⩾1 for which this remains true if K3t−1K_{3 t-1}K3t−1​ is replaced by K3t−2K_{3 t-2}K3t−2​ ?

(c) Now let t⩾2t \geqslant 2t⩾2. Suppose that the edges of the complete graph KnK_{n}Kn​ are coloured blue and yellow in such a way that there are a blue triangle and a yellow triangle with no vertices in common. Show that there are also a blue triangle and a yellow triangle that do have a vertex in common. Hence, or otherwise, show that whenever the edges of the complete graph K5tK_{5 t}K5t​ are coloured blue and yellow it must contain ttt monochromatic triangles, all of the same colour, with no two sharing a vertex. Is there any t⩾2t \geqslant 2t⩾2 for which this remains true if K5tK_{5 t}K5t​ is replaced by K5t−1K_{5 t-1}K5t−1​ ? [You may assume that whenever the edges of the complete graph K10K_{10}K10​ are coloured blue and yellow it must contain two monochromatic triangles of the same colour with no vertices in common.]
