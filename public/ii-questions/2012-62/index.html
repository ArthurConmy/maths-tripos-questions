Paper 2, Section II, H

#Galois Theory
2012

Let K,LK, LK,L be subfields of C\mathbb{C}C with K⊂LK \subset LK⊂L.

Suppose that KKK is contained in R\mathbb{R}R and L/KL / KL/K is a finite Galois extension of odd degree. Prove that LLL is also contained in R\mathbb{R}R.

Give one concrete example of K,LK, LK,L as above with K≠LK \neq LK=L. Also give an example in which KKK is contained in R\mathbb{R}R and L/KL / KL/K has odd degree, but is not Galois and LLL is not contained in R\mathbb{R}R.

[Standard facts on fields and their extensions can be quoted without proof, as long as they are clearly stated.]
