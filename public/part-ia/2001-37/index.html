2.I.4F

Probability
Part IA, 2001

The Ruritanian authorities decided to pardon and release one out of three remaining inmates, A,BA, BA,B and CCC, kept in strict isolation in the notorious Alkazaf prison. The inmates know this, but can't guess who among them is the lucky one; the waiting is agonising. A sympathetic, but corrupted, prison guard approaches AAA and offers to name, in exchange for a fee, another inmate (not A)A)A) who is doomed to stay. He says: "This reduces your chances to remain here from 2/32 / 32/3 to 1/21 / 21/2 : will it make you feel better?" AAA hesitates but then accepts the offer; the guard names BBB.

Assume that indeed BBB will not be released. Determine the conditional probability

P(A remains ∣B named )=P(A&B remain )P(B named )P(A \text { remains } \mid B \text { named })=\frac{P(A \& B \text { remain })}{P(B \text { named })} P(A remains ∣B named )=P(B named )P(A&B remain )​

and thus check the guard's claim, in three cases:

a) when the guard is completely unbiased (i.e., names any of BBB and CCC with probability 1/21 / 21/2 if the pair B,CB, CB,C is to remain jailed),

b) if he hates BBB and would certainly name him if BBB is to remain jailed,

c) if he hates CCC and would certainly name him if CCC is to remain jailed.
