2.II.12F

Probability
Part IA, 2001

A taxi travels between four villages, W,X,Y,ZW, X, Y, ZW,X,Y,Z, situated at the corners of a rectangle. The four roads connecting the villages follow the sides of the rectangle; the distance from WWW to XXX and YYY to ZZZ is 5 miles and from WWW to ZZZ and YYY to X10X 10X10 miles. After delivering a customer the taxi waits until the next call then goes to pick up the new customer and takes him to his destination. The calls may come from any of the villages with probability 1/41 / 41/4 and each customer goes to any other village with probability 1/31 / 31/3. Naturally, when travelling between a pair of adjacent corners of the rectangle, the taxi takes the straight route, otherwise (when it travels from WWW to YYY or XXX to ZZZ or vice versa) it does not matter. Distances within a given village are negligible. Let DDD be the distance travelled to pick up and deliver a single customer. Find the probabilitites that DDD takes each of its possible values. Find the expected value EDE DED and the variance Var DDD.
