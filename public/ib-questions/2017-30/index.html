Paper 2, Section I, E

#Groups, Rings and Modules
2017

(a) Define what is meant by a unique factorisation domain and by a principal ideal domain. State Gauss's lemma and Eisenstein's criterion, without proof.

(b) Find an example, with justification, of a ring RRR and a subring SSS such that

(i) RRR is a principal ideal domain, and

(ii) SSS is a unique factorisation domain but not a principal ideal domain.
