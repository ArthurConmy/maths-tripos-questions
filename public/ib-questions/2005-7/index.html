1.II.10C

#Groups, Rings and Modules
2005

Let GGG be a group, and HHH a subgroup of finite index. By considering an appropriate action of GGG on the set of left cosets of HHH, prove that HHH always contains a normal subgroup KKK of GGG such that the index of KKK in GGG is finite and divides nnn !, where nnn is the index of HHH in GGG.

Now assume that GGG is a finite group of order pqp qpq, where ppp and qqq are prime numbers with p<qp<qp<q. Prove that the subgroup of GGG generated by any element of order qqq is necessarily normal.
