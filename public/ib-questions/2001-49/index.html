2.II.14E

#Numerical Analysis
2001

(a) Let BBB be an n×nn \times nn×n positive-definite, symmetric matrix. Define the Cholesky factorization of BBB and prove that it is unique.

(b) Let AAA be an m×nm \times nm×n matrix, m⩾nm \geqslant nm⩾n, such that rank⁡A=n\operatorname{rank} A=nrankA=n. Prove the uniqueness of the "skinny QR factorization"

A=QR,A=Q R, A=QR,

where the matrix QQQ is m×nm \times nm×n with orthonormal columns, while RRR is an n×nn \times nn×n upper-triangular matrix with positive diagonal elements.

[Hint: Show that you may choose RRR as a matrix that features in the Cholesky factorization of B=ATAB=A^{T} AB=ATA.]
