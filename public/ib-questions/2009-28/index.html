Paper 4, Section II, G

#Geometry
2009

Let U⊂R2U \subset \mathbb{R}^{2}U⊂R2 be an open set. Let Σ⊂R3\Sigma \subset \mathbb{R}^{3}Σ⊂R3 be a surface locally given as the graph of an infinitely-differentiable function f:U→Rf: U \rightarrow \mathbb{R}f:U→R. Compute the Gaussian curvature of Σ\SigmaΣ in terms of fff.

Deduce that if Σ^⊂R3\widehat{\Sigma} \subset \mathbb{R}^{3}Σ⊂R3 is a compact surface without boundary, its Gaussian curvature is not everywhere negative.

Give, with brief justification, a compact surface Σ^⊂R3\widehat{\Sigma} \subset \mathbb{R}^{3}Σ⊂R3 without boundary whose Gaussian curvature must change sign.
