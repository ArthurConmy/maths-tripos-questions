Paper 2, Section II, E

#Linear Algebra
2015

(i) Suppose AAA is a matrix that does not have −1-1−1 as an eigenvalue. Show that A+IA+IA+I is non-singular. Further, show that AAA commutes with (A+I)−1(A+I)^{-1}(A+I)−1.

(ii) A matrix AAA is called skew-symmetric if AT=−AA^{T}=-AAT=−A. Show that a real skewsymmetric matrix does not have −1-1−1 as an eigenvalue.

(iii) Suppose AAA is a real skew-symmetric matrix. Show that U=(I−A)(I+A)−1U=(I-A)(I+A)^{-1}U=(I−A)(I+A)−1 is orthogonal with determinant 1 .

(iv) Verify that every orthogonal matrix UUU with determinant 1 which does not have −1-1−1 as an eigenvalue can be expressed as (I−A)(I+A)−1(I-A)(I+A)^{-1}(I−A)(I+A)−1 where AAA is a real skew-symmetric matrix.
