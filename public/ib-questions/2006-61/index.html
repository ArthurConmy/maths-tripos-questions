4.II.18A

#Fluid Dynamics
2006

A rectangular tank has a horizontal base and vertical sides. Viewed from above, the cross-section of the tank is a square of side aaa. At rest, the depth of water in the tank is hhh. Suppose that the free-surface is disturbed in such a way that the flow in the water is irrotational. Take the pressure at the free surface as atmospheric. Starting from the appropriate non-linear expressions, obtain free-surface boundary conditions for the velocity potential appropriate for small-amplitude disturbances of the surface.

Show that the governing equations and boundary conditions admit small-amplitude normal mode solutions for which the free-surface elevation above its equilibrium level is everywhere proportional to eiωte^{i \omega t}eiωt, and find the frequencies, ω\omegaω, of such modes.
