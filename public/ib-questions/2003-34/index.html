#Linear Mathematics
2003

Let α\alphaα be an endomorphism of a finite-dimensional real vector space UUU and let β\betaβ be another endomorphism of UUU that commutes with α\alphaα. If λ\lambdaλ is an eigenvalue of α\alphaα, show that β\betaβ maps the kernel of α−λι\alpha-\lambda \iotaα−λι into itself, where ι\iotaι is the identity map. Suppose now that α\alphaα is diagonalizable with nnn distinct real eigenvalues where n=dim⁡Un=\operatorname{dim} Un=dimU. Prove that if there exists an endomorphism β\betaβ of UUU such that α=β2\alpha=\beta^{2}α=β2, then λ⩾0\lambda \geqslant 0λ⩾0 for all eigenvalues λ\lambdaλ of α\alphaα.
