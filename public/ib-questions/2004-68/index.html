4.II.11E

#Linear Algebra
2004

(i) Let VVV be an nnn-dimensional inner-product space over C\mathbb{C}C and let α:V→V\alpha: V \rightarrow Vα:V→V be a Hermitian linear map. Prove that VVV has an orthonormal basis consisting of eigenvectors of α\alphaα.

(ii) Let β:V→V\beta: V \rightarrow Vβ:V→V be another Hermitian map. Prove that αβ\alpha \betaαβ is Hermitian if and only if αβ=βα\alpha \beta=\beta \alphaαβ=βα.

(iii) A Hermitian map α\alphaα is positive-definite if ⟨αv,v⟩>0\langle\alpha v, v\rangle>0⟨αv,v⟩>0 for every non-zero vector vvv. If α\alphaα is a positive-definite Hermitian map, prove that there is a unique positivedefinite Hermitian map β\betaβ such that β2=α\beta^{2}=\alphaβ2=α.
