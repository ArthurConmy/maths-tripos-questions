Paper 1, Section II, E

#Metric and Topological Spaces
2017

Consider R\mathbb{R}R and R2\mathbb{R}^{2}R2 with their usual Euclidean topologies.

(a) Show that a non-empty subset of R\mathbb{R}R is connected if and only if it is an interval. Find a compact subset K⊂RK \subset \mathbb{R}K⊂R for which R\K\mathbb{R} \backslash KR\K has infinitely many connected components.

(b) Let TTT be a countable subset of R2\mathbb{R}^{2}R2. Show that R2\T\mathbb{R}^{2} \backslash TR2\T is path-connected. Deduce that R2\mathbb{R}^{2}R2 is not homeomorphic to R\mathbb{R}R.
