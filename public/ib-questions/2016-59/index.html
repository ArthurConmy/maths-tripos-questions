k1=f(tn,yn),k2=f(tn+h2,yn+h2k1),yn+1=yn+hk2,\mathbf{k}_{1}=\mathbf{f}\left(t_{n}, \mathbf{y}_{n}\right), \quad \mathbf{k}_{2}=\mathbf{f}\left(t_{n}+\frac{h}{2}, \mathbf{y}_{n}+\frac{h}{2} \mathbf{k}_{1}\right), \quad \mathbf{y}_{n+1}=\mathbf{y}_{n}+h \mathbf{k}_{2}, k1​=f(tn​,yn​),k2​=f(tn​+2h​,yn​+2h​k1​),yn+1​=yn​+hk2​,

where hhh is the step size and tn=nht_{n}=n htn​=nh. Show that the order of this scheme is at least two. For this scheme, find the intersection of the linear stability domain with the real axis. Hence show that this method is not A-stable.
