Paper 4, Section II, 11G

#Groups, Rings and Modules
2013

Let RRR be an integral domain, and MMM be a finitely generated RRR-module.

(i) Let SSS be a finite subset of MMM which generates MMM as an RRR-module. Let TTT be a maximal linearly independent subset of SSS, and let NNN be the RRR-submodule of MMM generated by TTT. Show that there exists a non-zero r∈Rr \in Rr∈R such that rx∈Nr x \in Nrx∈N for every x∈Mx \in Mx∈M.

(ii) Now assume MMM is torsion-free, i.e. rx=0r x=0rx=0 for r∈Rr \in Rr∈R and x∈Mx \in Mx∈M implies r=0r=0r=0 or x=0x=0x=0. By considering the map M→NM \rightarrow NM→N mapping xxx to rxr xrx for rrr as in (i), show that every torsion-free finitely generated RRR-module is isomorphic to an RRR-submodule of a finitely generated free RRR-module.
