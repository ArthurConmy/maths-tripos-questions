Paper 4, Section II, E

#Groups, Rings and Modules
2014

(a) Consider the four following types of rings: Principal Ideal Domains, Integral Domains, Fields, and Unique Factorisation Domains. Arrange them in the form A⟹A \LongrightarrowA⟹ B⟹C⟹DB \Longrightarrow C \Longrightarrow DB⟹C⟹D (where A⟹BA \Longrightarrow BA⟹B means if a ring is of type AAA then it is of type BBB )

Prove that these implications hold. [You may assume that irreducibles in a Principal Ideal Domain are prime.] Provide examples, with brief justification, to show that these implications cannot be reversed.

(b) Let RRR be a ring with ideals III and JJJ satisfying I⊆JI \subseteq JI⊆J. Define KKK to be the set {r∈R:rJ⊆I}\{r \in R: r J \subseteq I\}{r∈R:rJ⊆I}. Prove that KKK is an ideal of RRR. If JJJ and KKK are principal, prove that III is principal.
