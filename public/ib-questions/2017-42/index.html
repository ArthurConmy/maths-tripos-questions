Paper 3, Section II, F

#Linear Algebra
2017

Let fff be a quadratic form on a finite-dimensional real vector space VVV. Prove that there exists a diagonal basis for fff, meaning a basis with respect to which the matrix of fff is diagonal.

Define the rank rrr and signature sss of fff in terms of this matrix. Prove that rrr and sss are independent of the choice of diagonal basis.

In terms of r,sr, sr,s, and the dimension nnn of VVV, what is the greatest dimension of a subspace on which fff is zero?

Now let fff be the quadratic form on R3\mathbb{R}^{3}R3 given by f(x,y,z)=x2−y2f(x, y, z)=x^{2}-y^{2}f(x,y,z)=x2−y2. For which points vvv in R3\mathbb{R}^{3}R3 is it the case that there is some diagonal basis for fff containing vvv ?
