4.II.12A

#Geometry
2007

Write down the Riemannian metric for the upper half-plane model H\mathbf{H}H of the hyperbolic plane. Describe, without proof, the group of isometries of H\mathbf{H}H and the hyperbolic lines (i.e. the geodesics) on H\mathbf{H}H.

Show that for any two hyperbolic lines ℓ1,ℓ2\ell_{1}, \ell_{2}ℓ1​,ℓ2​, there is an isometry of H\mathbf{H}H which maps ℓ1\ell_{1}ℓ1​ onto ℓ2\ell_{2}ℓ2​.

Suppose that ggg is a composition of two reflections in hyperbolic lines which are ultraparallel (i.e. do not meet either in the hyperbolic plane or at its boundary). Show that ggg cannot be an element of finite order in the group of isometries of H\mathbf{H}H.

[Existence of a common perpendicular to two ultraparallel hyperbolic lines may be assumed. You might like to choose carefully which hyperbolic line to consider as a common perpendicular.]
