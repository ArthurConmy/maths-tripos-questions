#Linear Mathematics

Let VVV be a vector space over R\mathbb{R}R. Let α:V→V\alpha: V \rightarrow Vα:V→V be a nilpotent endomorphism of VVV, i.e. αm=0\alpha^{m}=0αm=0 for some positive integer mmm. Prove that α\alphaα can be represented by a strictly upper-triangular matrix (with zeros along the diagonal). [You may wish to consider the subspaces ker⁡(αj)\operatorname{ker}\left(\alpha^{j}\right)ker(αj) for j=1,…,mj=1, \ldots, mj=1,…,m.]

Show that if α\alphaα is nilpotent, then αn=0\alpha^{n}=0αn=0 where nnn is the dimension of VVV. Give an example of a 4×44 \times 44×4 matrix MMM such that M4=0M^{4}=0M4=0 but M3≠0M^{3} \neq 0M3=0.

Let AAA be a nilpotent matrix and III the identity matrix. Prove that I+AI+AI+A has all eigenvalues equal to 1 . Is the same true of (I+A)(I+B)(I+A)(I+B)(I+A)(I+B) if AAA and BBB are nilpotent? Justify your answer.
