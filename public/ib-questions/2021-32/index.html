Paper 2, Section II, G

#Groups, Rings and Modules
2021

Let MMM be a module over a ring RRR and let S⊂MS \subset MS⊂M. Define what it means that SSS freely generates MMM. Show that this happens if and only if for every RRR-module NNN, every function f:S→Nf: S \rightarrow Nf:S→N extends uniquely to a homomorphism ϕ:M→N\phi: M \rightarrow Nϕ:M→N.

Let MMM be a free module over a (non-trivial) ring RRR that is generated (not necessarily freely) by a subset T⊂MT \subset MT⊂M of size mmm. Show that if SSS is a basis of MMM, then SSS is finite with ∣S∣⩽m|S| \leqslant m∣S∣⩽m. Hence, or otherwise, deduce that any two bases of MMM have the same number of elements. Denoting this number rk⁡M\operatorname{rk} MrkM and by quoting any result you need, show that if RRR is a Euclidean Domain and NNN is a submodule of MMM, then NNN is free with rk⁡N⩽rk⁡M\operatorname{rk} N \leqslant \operatorname{rk} MrkN⩽rkM.

State the Primary Decomposition Theorem for a finitely generated module MMM over a Euclidean Domain RRR. Deduce that any finite subgroup of the multiplicative group of a field is cyclic.
