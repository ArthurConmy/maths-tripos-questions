#Metric and Topological Spaces
2018

What does it mean to say that a topological space is compact? Prove directly from the definition that [0,1][0,1][0,1] is compact. Hence show that the unit circle S1⊂R2S^{1} \subset \mathbb{R}^{2}S1⊂R2 is compact, proving any results that you use. [You may use without proof the continuity of standard functions.]

The set R2\mathbb{R}^{2}R2 has a topology T\mathcal{T}T for which the closed sets are the empty set and the finite unions of vector subspaces. Let XXX denote the set R2\{0}\mathbb{R}^{2} \backslash\{0\}R2\{0} with the subspace topology induced by T\mathcal{T}T. By considering the subspace topology on S1⊂R2S^{1} \subset \mathbb{R}^{2}S1⊂R2, or otherwise, show that XXX is compact.
