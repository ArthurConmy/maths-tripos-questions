Paper 2, Section II, G

Coding and Cryptography
Part II, 2015

Briefly describe the RSAR S ARSA public key cipher.

Just before it went into liquidation, the Internet Bank decided that it wanted to communicate with each of its customers using an RSA cipher. So, it chose a large modulus NNN, which is the product of two large prime numbers, and chose encrypting exponents eje_{j}ej​ and decrypting exponents djd_{j}dj​ for each customer jjj. The bank published NNN and eje_{j}ej​ and sent the decrypting exponent djd_{j}dj​ secretly to customer jjj. Show explicitly that the cipher can be broken by each customer.

The bank sent out the same message to each customer. I am not a customer of the bank but have two friends who are and I notice that their published encrypting exponents are coprime. Explain how I can find the original message. Can I break the cipher?
