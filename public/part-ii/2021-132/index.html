Representation Theory
Part II, 2021

Let GGG be a finite group and work over C\mathbb{C}C.

(a) Let χ\chiχ be a faithful character of GGG, and suppose that χ(g)\chi(g)χ(g) takes precisely rrr different values as ggg varies over all the elements of GGG. Show that every irreducible character of GGG is a constituent of one of the powers χ0,χ1,…,χr−1\chi^{0}, \chi^{1}, \ldots, \chi^{r-1}χ0,χ1,…,χr−1. [Standard properties of the Vandermonde matrix may be assumed if stated correctly.]

(b) Assuming that the number of irreducible characters of GGG is equal to the number of conjugacy classes of GGG, show that the irreducible characters of GGG form a basis of the complex vector space of all class functions on GGG. Deduce that g,h∈Gg, h \in Gg,h∈G are conjugate if and only if χ(g)=χ(h)\chi(g)=\chi(h)χ(g)=χ(h) for all characters χ\chiχ of GGG.

(c) Let χ\chiχ be a character of GGG which is not faithful. Show that there is some irreducible character ψ\psiψ of GGG such that ⟨χn,ψ⟩=0\left\langle\chi^{n}, \psi\right\rangle=0⟨χn,ψ⟩=0 for all integers n⩾0n \geqslant 0n⩾0.
