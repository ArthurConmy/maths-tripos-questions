Paper 3, Section I, I

Coding and Cryptography
Part II, 2020

Let NNN and ppp be very large positive integers with ppp a prime and p>Np>Np>N. The Chair of the Committee is able to inscribe pairs of very large integers on discs. The Chair wishes to inscribe a collection of discs in such a way that any Committee member who acquires rrr of the discs and knows the prime ppp can deduce the integer NNN, but owning r−1r-1r−1 discs will give no information whatsoever. What strategy should the Chair follow?

[You may use without proof standard properties of the determinant of the r×rr \times rr×r Vandermonde matrix.]
