2.II.19H

Representation Theory
Part II, 2007

Let GGG be a finite group and let ZZZ be its centre. Show that if ρ\rhoρ is a complex irreducible representation of GGG, assumed to be faithful (that is, the kernel of ρ\rhoρ is trivial), then ZZZ is cyclic.

Now assume that GGG is a p-group (that is, the order of GGG is a power of the prime p)p)p), and assume that ZZZ is cyclic. If ρ\rhoρ is a faithful representation of GGG, show that some irreducible component of ρ\rhoρ is faithful.

[You may use without proof the fact that, since GGG is a p-group, ZZZ is non-trivial and any non-trivial normal subgroup of GGG intersects ZZZ non-trivially.]

Deduce that a finite ppp-group has a faithful irreducible representation if and only if its centre is cyclic.
