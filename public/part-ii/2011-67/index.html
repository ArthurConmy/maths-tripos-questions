Paper 1, Section I, G

Geometry and Groups
Part II, 2011

Let GGG be a finite subgroup of SO(3)\mathrm{SO}(3)SO(3) and let Ω\OmegaΩ be the set of unit vectors that are fixed by some non-identity element of GGG. Show that the group GGG permutes the unit vectors in Ω\OmegaΩ and that Ω\OmegaΩ has at most three orbits. Describe these orbits when GGG is the group of orientation-preserving symmetries of a regular dodecahedron.
