3.II.23H

Differential Geometry
Part II, 2007

(i) Let f:X→Yf: X \rightarrow Yf:X→Y be a smooth map between manifolds without boundary. Define critical point, critical value and regular value. State Sard's theorem.

(ii) Explain how to define the degree modulo 2 of a smooth map fff, indicating clearly the hypotheses on XXX and YYY. Show that a smooth map with non-zero degree modulo 2 must be surjective.

(iii) Let SSS be the torus of revolution obtained by rotating the circle (y−2)2+z2=1(y-2)^{2}+z^{2}=1(y−2)2+z2=1 in the yzy zyz-plane around the zzz-axis. Describe the critical points and the critical values of the Gauss map NNN of SSS. Find the degree modulo 2 of NNN. Justify your answer by means of a sketch or otherwise.
