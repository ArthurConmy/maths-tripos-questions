Prove the Borsuk-Ulam theorem in dimension 2: there is no map $f: S^{2} \rightarrow S^{1}$ such that $f(-x)=-f(x)$ for every $x \in S^{2}$. Deduce that $S^{2}$ is not homeomorphic to any subset of $\mathbb{R}^{2}$.
