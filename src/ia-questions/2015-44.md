(i) Consider the map from $\mathbb{R}^{4}$ to $\mathbb{R}^{3}$ represented by the matrix

$$\left(\begin{array}{rrrr}
\alpha & 1 & 1 & -1 \\
2 & -\alpha & 0 & -2 \\
-\alpha & 2 & 1 & 1
\end{array}\right)$$

where $\alpha \in \mathbb{R}$. Find the image and kernel of the map for each value of $\alpha$.
